 IDEA:
 You and your copilot have been entrusted with the important task of finding habitable parallel worlds.

 Earth high command gave you notice that the pilot that finds more habitable worlds will be promoted.
 Cognisant of the incentive structure placed before you, you notice that in addition to finding as
 many habitable worlds as possible, you need to prevent your copilot from finding as many as possible.

 You and your copilot made a bet who will find more habitable worlds. You notice, that to win this bet
 you not only need to find as many habitable worlds yourself, but also need to prevent your copilot from
 finding as many habitable worlds as possible.
  => So you win each mission if either:
      - you can land on a habitable world.
      - or your copilot fails to land on a habitable world on their shift.


SpaceShip metaphor:
 - both players start in same room at the beginning.
 - as the first game step both enter their respective adjacent room (attackers/defenders side)
 - then in each time step both players advance to an adjacent room, performing the actions they encounter.
   (I thought that one could maybe say that a fire broke out in the first room, spreading one tile
   with each timestep. Thus forcing the players to move forward.)


To simplify victory conditions for each possible landing, a safe room should be available.
OR: If we don't want to have to do this safe-room business, we can omit the fire breakout from the metaphor,
    if we find a solution to make it believable that the monster fails to win if it fails to land.
 => Victory condition metaphors:
     - [VICT] Defender lands (Monster in safe room): Defender can use exit capsule signal to warn civilization of Monster on spacestation.
     - [LOSS] Defender can't land (Monster in safe room): Defender dies, but monster survives.
     - [LOSS] Monster lands (Defender in safe room): Monster escaped while defender is stuck on spacestation.
     - [VICT] Monster can't land (Defender in safe room): Monster dies, but defender survives.

     Without saferoom guarantee:
     - [VICT] Defender lands (Monster burns): Defender survives, but monster doesn't.
     - [LOSS] Defender can't land (Monster burns): Defender dies, monster too.
     - [LOSS] Monster lands (Defender burns): Monster survives, but defender doesn't.
     - [VICT] Monster can't land (Defender burns): Monster dies, defender too.

     Without fire:
     - [VICT] Defender lands: Defender can use exit capsule signal to warn civilization of Monster on spacestation.
     - [LOSS] Defender can't land: Defender is stuck on spacestation with monster.
     - [LOSS] Monster lands: Monster escaped while defender is stuck on spacestation.
     - [VICT] Monster can't land: THIS IS A METAPHOR PROBLEM! FOR THIS REASON I INTRODUCED THE FIRE.

BUT, one last Problem:
 => We have limited space in the 2d grid I described. The space grows quadratically with manhattan distance,
    while the number of spaces needed for a formula grows exponentially with formula depth in the worst case.
    Still for our use case we won't be using very deeply nested or highly branched formulas, but might need
    to introduce empty rooms on both sides of the spacestation in order to create space. But I believe this
    should be manageable for examples as small as the puzzle formulas.
